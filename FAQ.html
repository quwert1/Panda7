Where can I find my VIN number?

The Vehicle Identification Number or VIN is composed of 17 characters (digits and capital letters) that act as a unique identifier for your vehicle. You can find your VIN on the driver’s side dashboard in your car, specifically near where the dashboard meets the edge of the windshield. You can also find your VIN on your vehicle registration card.

What is a “driver risk profile”?

Risk profiles are what insurance companies use to calculate how much money they should charge on your insurance premium. Among other factors, insurers take your vehicle, location, mileage and driving history into account when factoring your price. This is why two people driving the same car will likely not pay the same price.

What happens if I can’t remember all claims or tickets in the past 6 years?

Panda7 finds the best quotes based on your ticket and claim declaration. Once you purchase a policy, Panda7 will verify your declaration against official government records. Should a discrepancy occur, you will be notified with a price adjustment within the next business day. It is recommended to make sure no claims or tickets are missed. An incorrect declaration can lead to a fully declined policy in certain cases, or a price adjustment. Please note that parking tickets should not be declared.

Condo insurance

What is condo insurance?

Condo insurance is insurance you purchase that offers coverage to mend the gaps and exposures that the condominium association master policy may leave. While it depends on the amount of coverage you purchase and what the master policy already protects, your average condo policy may include coverage for your utilities and appliances, the interior walls of your individual unit, and your personal property. In addition to this, your condo insurance also protects your personal liability, in the event you are responsible for someone else’s injury or property damage.
